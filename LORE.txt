## 🪞 Lore / Origin

AetherField did not begin as an idea.

It began as a discrepancy.

While comparing charts across different sources, something impossible appeared:

> During a solar eclipse, the Sun and Moon were listed in different signs.

If a sign is understood as a region of the sky, this should not happen.
An eclipse is a direct alignment. The bodies occupy the same position, even if one outshines the other.

But across tools, sites, and traditions, the disagreement persisted.

---

That inconsistency led to a deeper question:

> *What does it actually mean for a body to be “in” a sign?*

Many systems fix the zodiac to a static frame, often anchored to a historical configuration. Over time, the sky drifts, but the framework remains locked.

AetherField takes a different approach.

Instead of fixing the wheel, it allows it to move.

It uses a sliding-scale model, where:

* the zodiac remains evenly divided
* positions are derived directly from spatial relationships
* alignments reflect where a body actually is, not where it was defined to be

This allows AetherField to determine the sign behind a body:

* even when its light overwhelms the stars
* even when it is below the horizon
* even when traditional references disagree

---

Calibration is part of this process.

An uncalibrated AetherField is functional, but approximate.
Like an initiate learning to read the sky, it improves as its dataset becomes more refined.

Local and hosted calibrations allow the system to converge:

* toward precision
* toward consistency
* toward something that can be trusted across time

---

AetherField does not replace existing systems.

It exists because something did not line up.

And once seen, it could not be unseen.
